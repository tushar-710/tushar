DR APJ Abdul Kalam

Great indian sceintist and politician who played a leading role in the developments of India's
missile and nuclear weapons programmes

About the legend

A.P.J Abdul kalam in full Avul pakir jainulabdeen abdul kalam, was born on october 15 1931,in Rameshwaram,Tamil Nadu,India

He served as the 11th president of India form 2002-2007

kalam earned a degree in aeronautical engeenering from the madras institute of technology and in 1958 joined the defence research and development organization (DRDO)

In 1969,he moved to the Indian Space Research organization where he was project director of SLV-III the first satelite lauch vehcile produced and deigned in India

rejoining DRDO in 1982,Kalam planned the program that produced a number of succesfull missiles,which helped earn him the nickname"Missile Man"

Among these successes was agni,Indias first intermidiate-range ballistic missile,which incorporated aspects of the SLV-III and was launched in 1989

He also played a pivotal organisational,technical,and political role in Indias Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998,the first since the original nuclear test by India in 1974

From 1992 to 1997 Kalam was sceintific advisor to the defence minister,and later he served as principal sceintific advisor(1999-2001)to the government with the rank of cabinet minister

In 1998 Kalam put forward a countrywide plan called Technology Vision 2020,whivh he described as a road map for transforming India from a less-developed to a developed soceity in 20 years.The plan called for,among other measures,increasing agricultural productivity,emphasizing technology as a vehicle for economic growth,and widening access to health care and education

Kalam received 7 honorary doctarates from 40 universities.The government of India honoured him with the Padma Bhushan in 1981 and and Bharat Ratna in 1997

While delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of management Shillong Kalam collapsed and died from an apparent cardiac arrest on 27 july 2015aged 83

Wheeler island,a national missile test site in odisha,was renamed Kalam Islandin september 2015

A prominent road in New Delhi was renamed from Aurangzeb Road to Dr Kalam Roadin august 2015

In february 2018,sceintists from the botanical Survey of India named a newly found plant species as Drypetes kalamii,in his honour
